Theorem 1.

Suppose a1,a2,… is a sequence of real or complex numbers. If the series

∑k=1∞ak

converges, then limk→∞⁡ak=0.

Remarks

  1. 1.

    The harmonic series ∑k=1∞1/k shows that the implication can not be reversed.

  2. 2.

    This result can be used as a first test for convergence of a series ∑k=1∞ak. If ak does not converge to 0, then ∑k=1∞ak does not converge either.

Proof.

Let S∈ℂ be the value of the sum, and let ε>0 be arbitrary. Then there exists an N≥1 such that

|∑k=1Mak-S|<ε2

for all M≥N. For j≥N we then have

|aj+1| = |∑k=1j+1ak-∑k=1jak|
≤ |∑k=1j+1ak-S|+|S-∑k=1jak|
< ε,

and the claim follows. ∎
