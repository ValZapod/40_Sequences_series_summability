Cooperative and noncooperative elliptic systems

Cooperative (+) and noncooperative (-) elliptic systems are of the form

(P±)⁢{-Δ⁢u=λ⁢u±δ⁢v+Gu⁢(x;u,v),x∈Ω-Δ⁢v=δ⁢u+γ⁢v±Gv⁢(x;u,v),x∈Ωu=v=0⁢or⁢∂⁡u∂⁡n=∂⁡v∂⁡n=0x∈∂⁡Ω

where Ω⊂ℝN(N≥1) is an open bounded domain, λ,γ,δ are real parameters, G⁢(x;u,v)∈𝒞1⁢(Ω¯×ℝ2;ℝ) in the variables (u,v)∈ℝ2 with ∇⁡G=(Gu,Gv). P+ and P- are called cooperative and noncooperative, respectively. In addition, δ is assumed to be positive for the noncooperative case. Note that systems (P±) are closely related to reaction-diffusion systems arising in various chemical/physical and biological phenomena.
