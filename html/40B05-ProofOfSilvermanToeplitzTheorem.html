proof of Silverman-Toeplitz theorem


First, we shall show that the series ∑n=0∞am⁢n⁢zn converges. Since the sequence {zn} converges, it must be bounded in absolute value — there must exist a constant K>0 such that |zn|≤K for all n. Hence, |am⁢n⁢zn|≤K⁢|am⁢n|. Summing this gives

∑n=0∞|am⁢n⁢zn|≤K⁢∑n=0∞|am⁢n|≤K⁢B.

Hence, the series ∑n=0∞am⁢n⁢zn is absolutely convergent which, in turn, implies that it converges.

Let z denote the limit of the sequence {zn} as n→∞. Then |z|≤K. We need to show that, for every ϵ>0, there exists an integer M such that

|∑n=0∞am⁢n⁢zn-z|<ϵ

whenever m>M.

Since the sequence {zn} converges, there must exist an integer n1 such that |zn-z|<ϵ4⁢B whenever n>n1.

By condition 3, there must exist constants mo,m1,…,mn1 such that

|am⁢n|<ϵ4⁢(n1+1)⁢(K+1) for 0≤n≤n1 and m>mn. (1)

Choose m′=max⁡{m0,m1,…,mn1}. Then

|∑n=0n1am⁢n⁢zn|≤∑n=0n1|am⁢n⁢zn|<∑n=0n1|zn|⁢ϵ4⁢(n1+1)⁢(K+1)<ϵ4 (2)

when m>m′.

By condition 2, there exists a constant m′′ such that

|∑n=0∞am⁢n-1|<ϵ6⁢(|z|+1)

whenever m>m′′. By (1),

|∑n=0n1am⁢n|<ϵ4⁢(K+1)≤ϵ4⁢(|z|+1)

when m>m′. Hence, if m>m′ and m>m′′, we have

|∑n=n1+1∞am⁢n-1|<ϵ2⁢(|z|+1). (3)

and

|∑n=n1+1∞am⁢n⁢(zn-z)|≤∑n=n1+1∞|am⁢n|⁢|zn-z|<ϵ4⁢B⁢∑n=0∞|am⁢n|≤ϵ4. (4)

By the triangle inequality and (2), (3), (4) it follows that

|∑n=0∞am⁢n⁢zn-z|≤|∑n=0n1am⁢n⁢zn|+|∑n=n1+1∞am⁢n⁢(zn-z)|+|z|⁢|∑n=n1+1∞am⁢n-1|
<ϵ4+ϵ4+ϵ2=ϵ.
