multiplicative cocycle


Let f:M→M be a measurable transformation, and let μ be an invariant probability measure. Consider A:M→G⁢L⁢(d,𝐑), a measurable transformation, where GL(d,R) is the space of invertible square matrices of size d. We define A-1:M→G⁢L⁢(d,𝐑) by A-1⁢(x)=[A⁢(x)]-1. Then we define the sequence of functions:

ϕn⁢(x)=A⁢(fn-1⁢(x))⁢⋯⁢A⁢(f⁢(x))⁢A⁢(x)
ϕ-n⁢(x)=[ϕn⁢(f-n⁢(x))]-1

for n≥1 and x∈M.

It is easy to verify that:

ϕm+n⁢(x)=ϕn⁢(fm⁢(x))⁢ϕm⁢(x)

for n,m∈𝐙 and x∈M.

The sequence (ϕn)n is called a multiplicative cocycle, or just cocycle defined by the transformation A.
