slower convergent series


Theorem.

If

a1+a2+a3+⋯ (1)

is a converging series with positive , then one can always form another converging series

g1+g2+g3+⋯

such that

limn→∞⁡gnan=∞ (2)

Proof.  Let S be the sum of (1),  Sn=a1+a2+⋯+an  the nth partial sum of (1) and  Rn+1=S-Sn=an+1+an+2+⋯  the corresponding remainder term.  Then we have

an=Rn-Rn+1=(Rn+Rn+1)⁢(Rn-Rn+1).

We set

gn:=anRn+Rn+1=Rn-Rn+1 ∀n=1, 2, 3,…

Then the series  g1+g2+g3+⋯  fulfils the requirements in the theorem.  Its gn are positive.  Further, it converges because its nth partial sum is equal to R1-Rn+1 which tends to the limit  R1=S  as  n→∞  since  Rn+1→0;  this implies also (2).
