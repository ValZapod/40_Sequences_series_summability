limit of sequence as sum of series


If U is the limit of a sequence

u1,u2,u3,…

of real or complex numbers, then U can be expressed as the series sum

U=u1+∑i=1∞(ui+1-ui).

Proof. Let  sn:=u1+∑i=1n-1(ui+1-ui).  We see that

sn=u1+∑i=1n-1ui+1-∑i=1n-1ui=u1+∑j=2nuj-∑i=1n-1ui=un

for all  n=1, 2, 3,…  Thus

u1+∑i=1∞(ui+1-ui)=limn→∞⁡sn=limn→∞⁡un=U,

Q.E.D.
