proof of ratio test


Assume k<1. By definition ∃N such that
n>N⟹|an+1an-k|<1-k2⟹|an+1an|<1+k2<1

i.e. eventually the series |an| becomes less than a convergent geometric series, therefore a shifted subsequence of |an| converges by the comparison test. Note that a general sequence bn converges iff a shifted subsequence of bn converges. Therefore, by the absolute convergence theorem, the series an converges.

Similarly for k>1 a shifted subsequence of |an| becomes greater than a geometric series tending to ∞, and so also tends to ∞. Therefore an diverges.
