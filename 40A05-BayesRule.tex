\noindent {\large {\bf \ Theorem:}}
\vspace{0.3cm}
\renewcommand{\theequation}{1.\arabic{equation}}

Let $\mu$ and $\nu$ be two probability measures on a measurable space $(\Omega, \mathcal{G})$ such that


$$d\nu(\omega) = f(\o) d\mu(\o) $$

for some $ f \in L^{1}(\mu)$.
Let $X$ be a random variable on $(\Omega, \mathcal{G})$ such that 

$$E_{\nu}[|X|]= \int_{\Omega}|X(\o)| f(\o)d\mu(\o) < \infty $$
 
($ \nu $-integrable)

Let $\mathcal{H}$ be a $\sigma$-algebra, $ \mathcal{H} \subset \mathcal{G}$. Then, 

$$ E_{\nu}[X|\mathcal{H}].E_{\mu}[f|\mathcal{H}]= E_{\mu}[fX|\mathcal{H}]  \quad a.s. $$

or
\begin{eqnarray*}
E_{\nu}[X|\mathcal{H}]=\frac{E_{\mu}[fX|\mathcal{H}]}{E_{\mu}[f|\mathcal{H}]} \quad a.s.
\end{eqnarray*}
